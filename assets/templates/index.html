Overview

Artificial Intelligence stands at the helm of the dramatic change in the management of illness in the health care sector. The ability of Artificial Intelligence to handle large-scale data analysis, thus aiding clinical decision-making, and automating diagnostic procedures portends new hope in facing global predictive care. According to current research on healthcare technology, LLM and artificial intelligence are instrumental components of medical services. With the aim of designing a more advanced healthcare system, using Large Language Models (LLM) to enhance medical diagnostics by individualised health management is one approach. It may help the user with supplements using the symptoms they give. An integrated system also includes offering you a medical chatbot to be your virtual health assistant. It analyses your health data using advanced AI and provides real-time answers to your questions. It will give you basic advice and can help prepare you for a further knowledgeable conversation with medical experts. It is accurate and offers personalized information based on your health profile; it is available anytime, anywhere to answer questions about symptoms or to request assistance from the medical world. It can be applied to anyone, regardless of age, from young adults to old people, due to its design. The general public will live healthier, better lives as a result of using the healthcare system.

Large-scale, complex architecture, and fabulous capability to generate language set the definition of LLMs, which include revolutionized advancements in the domain of NLP. It enables interpretation and generation of text similar to humans using deep neural network topologies, transformers, and huge quantities of training data. Consequently, it can be used as a resourceful tool in natural processing applications, such as chatbots, code generation, and summarisation.

Text summarization can be considered a highly complex task, as it requires understanding the main points of a piece of text and then condensing these points into a shorter form. LLMs have been applied to a wide range of additional tasks such as Sentiment Analysis in Customer Reviews, Spam Detection in Email Filters. For instance, an LLM can be utilized to decide whether some post on Twitter was written by a specific individual, or determine who wrote a particular piece of writing anonymously.

A new drug in the market is associated with drug development. The process of developing drugs, however, is very complex and time-consuming and is very costly while carrying low chances of success. It can rapidly produce drug candidates far more speedily than traditional drug discovery since it analyzes the chemical structure of successful drugs and simulates variations. Not only does this save time and resources, but it may have discovered some drugs that otherwise would have been left out by traditional methods.

It also decreases dependency on real-world data collection, reduces costs and time in collecting and preparing actual patient records. Furthermore, synthetic data with varying attributes and parameters can help physicians and researchers to explore and analyze a range of ideas [5, 3, 7], which, in turn, brings in new discoveries and understanding. Apart from improving the research capabilities, the synthetic data helps in data protection regulations compliance. It significantly reduces the chances of vulnerable patient information exposure. Such regulations are well in line with the ever-increasing practice of ethical AI, where technologies are also being developed so that innovations are balanced against privacy concerns.

Customized therapy for specific patients is important in personalized medical care. To develop individualized recommendations for therapy, LLMs will be required in gathering patient data. The large patient data sets can be trained on in order for the AI to begin recognizing patterns and relationships that a human physician may bypass. For instance, it might find that a specific drug would be a good fit for the patients who have a particular genetic signature. A customized treatment plan that is based on the requirements of each patient can then be made using this information.
